CPR Guide
Emergency Call

Call Emergency Services First!

Before starting CPR, ensure emergency services are on the way

Call 999
1

Check Response

Gently shake the person's shoulders and ask loudly:

"Are you okay?"

2

Check Breathing

Look for chest movement
Listen for breathing sounds
Feel for breath on your cheek
3

Chest Compressions

Place hands in center of chest
Push hard and fast (100-120 per minute)
Allow chest to fully recoil

Compression Rhythm

Follow the beat of "Stayin' Alive" - perfect tempo for compressions!

Important Notice

Ribs may break during CPR, especially in older adults. This is normal and the priority is saving a lifeâ€”proper technique helps minimize injury while ensuring effective compressions.

4

Rescue Breaths

Tilt head back gently
Pinch nose closed
Give 2 breaths (1 second each)

Watch for Chest Rise

Each breath should make the chest visibly rise. If not, reposition the head and try again.

5

Continue CPR Cycles

CPR Cycle Pattern
  • 30 chest compressions
  • 2 rescue breaths
  • Repeat until help arrives

When to Stop CPR

  • Emergency services arrive
  • Person shows signs of life
  • You're too exhausted to continue
  • Scene becomes unsafe

Remember

Your efforts can make the difference between life and death. Stay calm and focused. Emergency services are on their way to help.
